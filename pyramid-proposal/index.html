03 Layer one · the foundation

First: know
the business.

Layer one is the Pyramid Oracle: one knowledge base holding how the business actually works, its numbers, products, customers, brand and decisions. The dashboard, the content and the campaigns all read from it, so nothing downstream is working off a guess. Get it right once, keep it current, and everything on top of it stays accurate.

The Oracle a few months in: nine hubs, every note linked, and the records it accumulates, meetings, decisions, model pages, trading data, clustered around them. Tap a hub to see what is in it.

Why a web, and not a folder

Most businesses keep what they know in scattered docs, and it goes stale the moment it is saved. The Oracle is built as a web of linked notes instead: a hub for each part of the business (the nodes), joined to the notes around it (the spokes). It is a set of plain linked files in Obsidian, and it matters for three reasons.

Connected

Not scattered

A fact sits next to the product it describes, the customer who buys it and the decision behind it. The links are where the insight is, not the pages.

One source of truth

Change it once

Update a fact in its note and everything joined to it stays right. No more the same number saying three different things across docs, slides and email.

Built for AI

Reads in context

A web of linked notes is exactly what an AI works best from. Point the content engine or the dashboard at it and it answers in context, because the context is already wired in.

Ask it, right from Slack

The Oracle isn't a folder you go looking in. It sits in your Slack, and anyone on the team can ask it a question in plain English. It answers from the knowledge base and the trading data, and tells you where the answer came from.
